Our growth is your potential

EUG is a young growing team of dedicated individuals. Our dynamic approaches to garden propagation set EUG apart from typical landscapers. As an adaptive team, we find creative solutions to provide a guarantee of quality and value. Long term growth is important, seeing the flourish of life that a few plants can bring is motiving.

EUG makes it easy and fulfilling to give back. As gardeners, landscapes and farmers it's our duty to maintain healthy open greens spaces. This can have a high ecological impact when introducing natives and recommending alternatives to foreign invasives. Through creativity and simplicity EUG carves the path for a more sustainable future.

EUG has adopted the key elements of the U.S. Forest Service Native Plant policy, directly from their site:

✺Native plants are defined as all indigenous terrestrial and aquatic plant species that evolved naturally in a defined native ecosystem.

✺Under no circumstances will non-native invasive plant species be used as plant materials for restoration, rehabilitation, or reconstruction of native ecosystems.

✺The best information available should be used to choose genetically appropriate native plant materials for the site to be restored.

✺A reliable source of native plant materials, either as seed or other vegetative propagules is essential for the successful implementation of the native plant materials program.

✺Native plant materials will be the first choice in revegetation for restoration and rehabilitation of native ecosystems where timely natural regeneration of the native plant community will not occur.

✺Non‑native, non‑invasive plant species may be used when:

✽Needed in emergency conditions to protect basic resource values,

✽As an interim, non‑persistent measure designed to aid in the re‑establishment of native plants,

✽When native plant materials are not available, and

✽In permanently altered plant communities.
